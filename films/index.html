Written on 20 Mar 2026 at 15:50

i watched end of watch a few days ago and i really liked it, but i can’t help feeling like it sometimes drifts into something close to cop glorification. not in an obvious or intentional way, but more in how it frames things.

it feels like the movie focuses a lot on the bond between the two main characters, almost idealizing that kind of partnership. their relationship is probably strongest part of the film, but at times it seems to overshadow everything else, especially the more uncomfortable aspects of being a cop.

there are moments where the reality of the job feels exaggerated in one direction while other parts are left out entirely. like, the danger and intensity are very present, but the systemic issues within policing, or the consequences that might follow some of their actions, don’t really get explored. it’s as if the film is more interested in the emotional experience of the characters than in questioning the institution they’re part of.

even some of their interactions feel slightly constructed at times, like they’re meant to reinforce this idea of loyalty and brotherhood rather than show something more complex or contradictory.

i still think the movie works, especially in how it builds intimacy between the characters, but i keep feeling like there’s something missing underneath it all, something that the film chooses not to look at too closely.
